WHO YOU ARE NOW
Your role and path in AlphaX
🚀
Alphanaut

Like astronauts explore space, Alphanauts explore knowledge frontiers.

What that means:

  • You're an explorer of the intersection between global science and local impact
  • You contribute to translation, research, or training initiatives
  • You're part of a community building Syrian research capacity
  • You showed up, that already sets you apart

What's expected:

  • Time: ~6-10 hours/week (flexible around your life)
  • Participation: Active in your work area, present in meetings
  • Mindset: Ask questions, help others, communicate challenges

What you get:

  • Training in research methods, academic writing, technical skills
  • Your name on published work (papers, translations, tools)
  • Mentorship from published researchers
  • Community of ambitious, talented peers

REAL TALK
Questions you're actually wondering

Honest Answers to Real Questions

"How much time will this actually take?"
6-10 hours/week if actively contributing. Some weeks more (deadlines), some less (between projects). We respect your other commitments, just communicate.
"What if I don't know how to do something?"
That's literally the point. AlphaX is a training ground. Nobody expects you to arrive with all skills. Ask questions, watch tutorials, learn by doing, ask for help. Ignorance admitted is ignorance solved.
"What if I need to step back for exams/life?"
Tell your Navigator. We get it. Just don't ghost, communicate and we'll work it out. AlphaX will be here when you're ready.
"How do I know if I'm doing well?"
You'll get feedback in meetings. If something's off, we'll tell you kindly and early. No surprises. No passive-aggressive silence.
"Can I suggest new ideas/projects?"
YES. Bring ideas to meetings. Best ideas come from the team, not just founders. If you see a gap, say it. If you have a better way, propose it.
"What if this doesn't work out for me?"
No hard feelings. Better to realize early than drag it out. We'll help transition your work and stay friends. Not everyone fits everywhere.
"Will this actually help my career/future?"
Yes, if you do the work. Publications, skills, network, outputs—all real and valuable. But no guarantees of fame or funding. We promise growth and outputs, not viral success.
"What makes AlphaX different from other initiatives?"
We actually build things. We have published researchers leading. We focus on outputs, not just meetings and social media posts. We ship work, not promises.

DO ✅

  • Show your work (even incomplete)
  • Ask questions (even "dumb" ones)
  • Take ownership of tasks
  • Help others when you can
  • Give and receive feedback gracefully
  • Celebrate others' wins
  • Communicate early when stuck
  • Document your process
  • Think about impact, not just tasks
  • Have fun, this should be energizing

DON'T ❌

  • Ghost the team
  • Miss deadlines without communication
  • Hoard information
  • Perfectionism paralysis (done > perfect)
  • Take credit for others' work
  • Complain without suggesting solutions
  • Work in isolation—collaborate!

When Things Get Hard (Because They Will)

😰
Week 3 Crisis
Reality hits. More work than you thought. Behind on other stuff. Imposter syndrome screaming. → Normal. Talk to your Navigator. Adjust commitment. Remember why you joined. Everyone feels this. It passes.
😐
Month 2 Plateau
Initial excitement fades. Progress feels slow. "Does this even matter?" → Expected. Look at what you've actually created. Talk to other Alphanauts. Building infrastructure is slow, but it compounds. Trust the process.
💢
Conflict with Team
Disagreement on approach. Frustration with work style. Feeling unheard. → Human. Address it directly but kindly. Talk to Navigator if needed. Conflicts happen and can be resolved. We're building something together, not performing perfection.
❤️
Personal Crisis
Family, health, exams, life. → Tell someone. We'll support you. AlphaX will be here when you're ready. Your wellbeing > any project.

⚠️ What We Don't Tolerate

Hard lines:

  • Plagiarism or academic dishonesty
  • Taking credit for others' work
  • Harassment or discrimination of any kind
  • Sharing confidential information
  • Consistent ghosting without communication
  • Undermining team or spreading negativity

Consequence: Conversation → Warning → Removal if needed

Culture: We're understanding and flexible about mistakes and challenges. But we protect the team's integrity, safety, and culture. Non-negotiable.
